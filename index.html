05 · Critical Findings

The 5 Insights That Matter Most

Ranked by revenue impact. These are the findings any CEO should act on immediately.

1

97% of Customers Never Return

Only 2,801 of 93,358 customers made a second purchase. If Olist improved repeat rate to just 10%, that would add approximately R$1.2M+ in annual revenue without acquiring a single new customer. This is the highest-leverage metric in the entire business.

↑ Revenue potential: R$ 1.2M+
2

141% YoY Growth — Now Plateauing

Revenue grew from R$3.0M (Jan–Aug 2017) to R$7.2M (Jan–Aug 2018) — extraordinary. But H2 2018 data shows a plateau around R$850K–950K/month. Without intervention, the growth engine is stalling as top-of-funnel acquisition slows.

↑ YoY Growth: +141%
3

Late Deliveries Destroy Reviews (& Revenue)

8,714 orders arrived late (7.9%). Late delivery orders score an average of 2.55 vs. 4.21 for on-time — a 39% satisfaction collapse. Negative reviews suppress future conversions and seller ratings on the platform. Fixing logistics is a revenue decision.

Score drop: 4.21 → 2.55
4

Black Friday Is an Untapped Goldmine

November 2017 generated R$987K — a 52.4% spike over October. This was a single-year observation. A structured Black Friday + Cyber Monday strategy with personalized campaigns, inventory staging, and seller coordination could target R$1.5M+ in a single month.

Nov 2017 spike: +52.4% MoM
5

São Paulo Concentration = Geographic Risk

SP state alone contributes R$5.07M — 38% of total revenue. RJ and MG add another 25%. 63% of all revenue comes from 3 states. Expansion into RS, PR, SC, BA — where growth is visible but underdeveloped — represents R$2M+ in addressable opportunity.

SP alone: 38% of revenue
+

Freight Is 21% of Order Value — Hiding Margin

Customers pay R$22 in freight on a R$137 average order. This 21% freight burden suppresses conversion rates for mid-ticket items. A free-shipping threshold strategy (e.g., "free shipping over R$200") could lift AOV by 20–30% on the addressable basket.

Freight burden: 21.3% of total
06 · Strategic Playbook

Six High-Impact Recommendations

Ranked by ROI. Every recommendation is tied to a specific data signal. These are not theories — they are decisions.

Priority 1

Launch a Post-Purchase Retention Engine

Build an automated email/SMS sequence triggered 14, 30, and 60 days after first delivery. Use purchase category to power personalized cross-sell (e.g., Health & Beauty buyer → complementary product). Introduce a loyalty point system visible in the customer dashboard. Target: convert 97% one-time buyers down to 85% within 12 months.

R$1.2M+ Annual Revenue Uplift
Priority 2

Fix the Logistics Tail — 7.9% Late Rate Is Unacceptable

Identify the specific seller + state + category combinations driving the 8,714 late orders. Late deliveries score 2.55 — they generate negative reviews that suppress future sales. Implement SLA dashboards for sellers, with automatic notifications at 80% of delivery window. Consider penalizing chronic late sellers or deprioritizing them in search ranking.

+0.3★ Platform Rating Lift
Priority 3

Deploy a Structured Black Friday Strategy

The 2017 November spike (+52.4% MoM) was organic — not engineered. Build a coordinated campaign: seller inventory pre-staging in October, targeted email blasts to lapsed customers, category-specific flash sales (Health & Beauty, Watches, Sports), and a Cyber Monday extension. With coordinated execution, R$1.5M in a single month is achievable.

R$500K+ Incremental Nov. Revenue
Priority 4

Introduce Free Shipping Threshold to Lift AOV

Current AOV is R$137 product + R$22 freight. Test a "Free shipping on orders over R$200" threshold with a progress bar in cart. This incentivizes basket-building. Even if Olist absorbs 50% of freight cost on qualifying orders, the AOV lift from cart add-ons (R$137→R$200+) generates net positive margin. Start with top 5 categories.

+20–30% AOV on Eligible Orders
Priority 5

Geographic Expansion Into RS, PR, BA Markets

RS (R$729K), PR (R$666K), and BA (R$494K) are performing well but have low seller density versus SP. Partner with local logistics providers and recruit category-specific sellers in these states. A targeted seller acquisition campaign in these 3 states, combined with local promotion, could add R$1M+ in revenue with minimal incremental customer acquisition cost.

R$1M+ Regional Revenue Potential
Priority 6

Invest in Monday–Tuesday Paid Acquisition & Scale Down Weekend

Monday drives 14.9% of weekly orders, Saturday only 10.0% — a 49% gap. Shift paid media budget toward Mon/Tue targeting, especially peak purchase hours (10am–5pm and 8–10pm). Weekend campaigns should focus on re-engagement/retargeting rather than cold acquisition. This budget reallocation can improve ROAS by 20–35% with zero additional spend.

+25% Media Efficiency (ROAS)

Critical DAX Measures

Must-build calculations for the Power BI model

// Total Revenue
Total Rev = SUM(fact[price]) + SUM(fact[freight])

// AOV
AOV = DIVIDE([Total Rev], DISTINCTCOUNT(fact[order_id]))

// Repeat Rate
Repeat Rate = DIVIDE(CALCULATE(DISTINCTCOUNT(fact[customer_id]), FILTER(...orders>1)), DISTINCTCOUNT(fact[customer_id]))

// MoM Growth
MoM Growth = DIVIDE([Rev] - [Rev PrevMonth], [Rev PrevMonth])
08 · Executive Summary

The Verdict

For the CEO, COO, and Board. What matters, what to do, and in what order.

🚀 The Growth Is Real

Olist achieved 141% YoY revenue growth — from R$3.0M to R$7.2M in the same Jan–Aug window. This is not incremental. This is a platform finding product-market fit at scale. The foundation is strong.

⚠️ But Growth Is Slowing

Monthly revenue plateaued at R$850K–R$980K through H2 2018 after peaking in November 2017. Without a retention engine, the company is on a treadmill: spending to acquire customers who never come back.

💡 The Single Biggest Opportunity

A 97% one-time buyer rate means nearly every customer is an acquisition cost with no LTV. Converting even 10% to repeat buyers — through retention programs — is worth more than doubling the ad budget.

📦 Logistics = Revenue

7.9% late delivery rate causes review scores to collapse from 4.21 to 2.55. Bad reviews hurt seller credibility and suppress future orders. This is not an ops problem — it's a revenue problem.

🗺️ Geography Is Opportunity

63% of revenue from 3 states signals underdevelopment everywhere else. RS, PR, BA, and GO are showing genuine demand with under-served supply. Regional expansion is the next growth lever.

🏆 Health & Beauty Is the Star

Highest revenue (R$1.23M), high satisfaction (4.19★), and high repurchase potential. This is the category to double down on — more sellers, more SKUs, dedicated promotions, and subscription pilots.

The Strategic Imperative

Olist is at an inflection point. The explosive growth phase — driven by platform novelty and top-of-funnel acquisition — is maturing. The next chapter of growth will not be won by spending more on acquisition. It will be won by retaining the 97% of customers currently walking out the door after one purchase, fixing the logistics failures that destroy trust, and strategically expanding into the under-penetrated states where demand already exists. The data is clear: the tools for R$25M+ annual revenue are already in this dataset. The question is whether the organization moves fast enough to use them.
